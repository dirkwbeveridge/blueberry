Blueberry · A story in three chapters

Most apps watch you.
Blueberry sits with you.

Pregnancy apps tend to do one of two things: chase data like a fitness tracker, or fill your morning with feeds and forums. Blueberry is built for the two people actually living the pregnancy: her, and the person walking beside her. One household. Two phones. No audience.

What you see on each device is shaped by the role you play. Mom opens the Daily Companion: her week, her body, her notes to herself. Partner opens Couple OS: the same pregnancy from his side, framed as support. The data underneath is shared. The view is not.

A quieter way to walk through forty weeks, together.

01
Two views, one household
Designed for two people. Mom's view focuses inward. Partner's view focuses on her. Same source of truth, two different lenses.
02
Private by default
Mom's symptom logs and journals are hers. Partner sees only what she chose to share. Usually a sentence, a chip, a mood.
03
Quiet, not anxious
No streaks. No feeds. No comparisons. One focal week, one paced focus list, one upcoming appointment.
04
Grows with the family
When baby arrives, Blueberry shifts into Family Mode. Same household, new rhythm. None of your story is left behind.
